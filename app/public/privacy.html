Privacy Policy

Last updated 28 June 2026

Celestual (celestual.us) is an anonymous, reciprocal matching site: you enter your Instagram @ and the @ of someone you’re thinking about, and you only ever find out if it’s mutual. One-sided entries are never revealed to anyone. This policy explains exactly what we collect, how we use it, who processes it, and how you can delete it. The service is operated by Celestual LLC, a Delaware (USA) limited liability company, which is the data controller for the personal data described here. Reach us any time at privacy@celestual.us.

What we collect

  • Instagram handles. When you make an entry we store two handles — yours and the one you enter — so we can detect whether the same pairing is later entered in reverse. These are stored as part of an encrypted entry record.
  • Your email (optional). Only if you provide one, and only so we can notify you if an entry turns out to be mutual, or remind you. You can use Celestual without giving an email.
  • Identity-verification signal. To confirm an @ is really yours, you send a one-time code by Instagram DM to our account (@celestual.us). Meta’s messaging webhook tells our backend that the code arrived from that handle. We do not receive your Instagram password and never gain access to your Instagram account.
  • Basic technical data. Standard request/log data needed to serve the site securely (e.g. handled by our hosting and database providers). We do not run advertising or third-party tracking cookies.

How we use it

  • To compute whether an entry is mutual, and only then notify the earlier entrant.
  • To restore your own stars (“your sky”) when you sign back in with the same @.
  • To enforce a fair, server-side weekly entry budget and prevent abuse.
  • To verify, when required, that an @ belongs to the person using it.

What we never do

  • We never reveal a one-sided entry. There is no on-screen result for the person you entered.
  • We never post, message, follow, or alert the person you enter.
  • We never sell your data or share it for advertising.

Who processes your data

We use a small set of trusted service providers, each only for the function below:

  • Supabase — encrypted database and backend functions that store entries and the optional email.
  • Vercel — hosting and delivery of the website.
  • ManyChat & Meta Platforms (Instagram) — relay the one-time DM verification message and tell us the code was received from your handle.
  • Resend — sends the match / reminder email, when you’ve given one.

How long we keep it

  • Entries are kept only while they’re needed for matching: until you withdraw them, until the handle involved is removed and blocked, or until you delete your account — whichever comes first.
  • Verification records (the one-time DM codes) expire within minutes and expired records are pruned automatically.
  • Rate-limiting logs (short-lived operational records used to stop abuse) are pruned automatically on a rolling basis.
  • When you ask us to erase something, we delete it without undue delay.

If someone entered you

People can enter a handle that belongs to you without your involvement. Here is what that means for you: you will never be notified, contacted, or marketed to because of it, and no one — including the person who entered you — ever learns anything unless you independently enter them back. If you’d rather not exist on Celestual at all, you can remove and block your handle in one step, free, without creating an account. That erases every entry referencing your handle and prevents it from ever being entered again. This will always be free — it is the one promise this product cannot function without.

Your rights

Wherever you live, you can access, correct, export, or erase your data by emailing privacy@celestual.us or using the in-product tools. Additionally:

  • EU/EEA & UK (GDPR): you have the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection. We process entry data to provide the service you ask for (performance of a contract) and abuse-prevention data under our legitimate interest in keeping the service safe. You may lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.
  • California (CCPA/CPRA): you have the rights to know, delete, and correct. We do not sell personal information and do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising, so there is nothing to opt out of. We never discriminate against you for exercising your rights.
  • Data location: we’re a US company and data is processed in the United States by the providers below. Where EU/UK data is transferred, our providers rely on standard contractual clauses.

If we ever add paid features

Optional paid extras (see the Terms) would be processed by a dedicated payment processor — we would never see or store full card numbers, and this page will name the processor before anything is ever charged. Paid features never change what we collect about anyone.

Deleting your data

You can delete your data yourself at any time, and you can ask us to erase and block any handle — including your own, if someone entered you. Full, step-by-step instructions are here:
