Privacy Policy

Last updated 11 July 2026

Celestual (celestual.us) is a double-blind, mutual-reveal service: you enter the Instagram handle of someone on your mind (“placing a ping”), and both of you find out only if they independently enter you back — at the same moment. One-sided pings are never revealed to anyone. This policy explains exactly what we collect, how we use it, who processes it, and how anyone — user or not — can delete it. The service is operated by Celestual LLC, a Delaware (USA) limited liability company, the data controller for the personal data described here. Reach us any time at privacy@celestual.us.

What we collect — and what we deliberately can't read

  • Your handle. When you place a ping we store your own Instagram handle (verified as yours via the DM check below).
  • Who you ping — as a hash only. The handle you enter is stored exclusively as a salted one-way hash. Matching runs hash-to-hash. Neither we, nor anyone who ever breached our database, can read who your pings point at. The readable name exists only on your own device — and, once a pair turns mutual, in the match record both of you already know.
  • An optional intent line. If you attach one of the five fixed lines to a ping, we store its identifier. It is shown only to the other person, only at a mutual match.
  • Your email (optional). Only if you provide one, and only for two notes: a mutual match, and a heads-up before one of your own pings lapses. You can use celestual without giving an email.
  • Identity-verification signal. To confirm an @ is really yours, you send a one-time code by Instagram DM to our account (@celestual.us). Meta's messaging webhook tells our backend the code arrived from that handle. We never receive your Instagram password and never gain access to your Instagram account.
  • School email verification (optional). Joining a school community requires proving you're there: we email a one-time code to an address at that school's domain. We store the address you gave, the school it was for, a one-way hash of the code (never the code itself), and the requesting IP for rate-limiting. Codes expire within ten minutes and expired records are pruned automatically. The address is used only for this confirmation — it is never shown to anyone and never used for marketing.
  • Communities and campus preregistrations (optional). If you join a community, or preregister for a campus opening, we store your handle with that community or campus, plus your optional email for the opening note. Community counters are shown only in aggregate, and only once a community passes one hundred members.
  • Your public @ (optional, off by default). Your star in a community sky is anonymous. If you explicitly choose "announce your @," your own handle is shown above your star to people viewing that sky. It announces only that you're here — never who you pinged. You can turn it off again at any time, in one tap.
  • Basic technical data. Standard request/log data needed to serve the site securely (handled by our hosting and database providers). We do not run advertising or third-party tracking cookies.

How long a ping lives

  • Sixty days. Every ping stands for sixty days. Renewing it is free and unlimited; if you let it lapse, the record — hash and all — is purged automatically. Unresolved feelings do not accumulate here.
  • Retiring is immediate. "Let it go" deletes the ping at once and frees your slot. Nothing was ever revealed either way.
  • Mutual records persist so both people can return to them, until either person withdraws or opts out.
  • Verification codes expire within minutes and expired records are pruned automatically. Rate-limiting logs are short-lived and pruned on a rolling basis.

How we use it

  • To compute — by hash — whether a ping is mutual, and only then to tell exactly the two people involved.
  • To show you, and only you, the state of your own pings.
  • To enforce the three-slot rule, the sixty-day lapse, and the anti-abuse rate limits, server-side.
  • To verify that a handle belongs to the person using it before anything can match to it.
  • To confirm you're really at a school — by a one-time code emailed to an address at its domain — before you join its community.
  • To run campus openings: counting verified preregistrations toward a public threshold and mailing everyone at once when it trips.

What we never do

  • We never reveal a one-sided ping — not on screen, not by email, not by implication. There is no result of any kind for the person you entered.
  • We never post, message, follow, or alert the person you enter.
  • We never send anyone a message implying someone entered them. Every number we publish (counters, meters, campus aggregates) is exactly true or not shown.
  • We never sell your data or share it for advertising.

Who processes your data

A small set of service providers, each only for the function below:

  • Supabase — database and backend functions that store the records described above.
  • Vercel — hosting and delivery of the website.
  • ManyChat & Meta Platforms (Instagram) — relay the one-time DM verification message and tell us the code was received from your handle.
  • Resend — sends the match, lapse, campus-opening and school-verification-code emails, when you've given an address.

If your handle was entered

Someone can place a ping at your handle without your involvement. Here is what that means for you: you will never be notified, contacted, or marketed to because of it; your handle is stored only as a salted hash; and the record self-destructs within sixty days unless renewed. No one — including the person who placed it — ever learns anything unless you independently enter them back. If you'd rather not exist on celestual at all, you can opt your handle out in one step, free, without creating an account. That erases everything referencing your handle and makes it permanently un-pingable. This will always be free — it is the one promise this product cannot function without.

Your rights

Wherever you live, you can access, correct, export, or erase your data by emailing privacy@celestual.us or using the in-product tools. Additionally:

  • EU/EEA & UK (GDPR): you have the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection. We process ping data to provide the service you ask for (performance of a contract) and abuse-prevention data under our legitimate interest in keeping the service safe. Hashing and the sixty-day purge are our data-minimisation measures for third-party handles. You may lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.
  • California (CCPA/CPRA): you have the rights to know, delete, and correct. We do not sell personal information and do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising, so there is nothing to opt out of there. We never discriminate against you for exercising your rights.
  • Korea (PIPA): the same access, correction and deletion rights apply; the opt-out page is the fastest route, and email requests are honored regardless of where you live.
  • Data location: we're a US company and data is processed in the United States by the providers above. Where EU/UK data is transferred, our providers rely on standard contractual clauses.

Paid features

Celestual currently sells nothing — there are no subscriptions, tiers, or purchases anywhere in the product. If a paid extra ever ships (the only one contemplated is a one-time fourth ping slot), a dedicated payment processor would be named on this page before anything is charged, we would never see or store full card numbers, and nothing paid will ever change what we collect about anyone or what stays free (see the Terms).
